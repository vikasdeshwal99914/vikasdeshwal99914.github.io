History of Brooms
Ancient Times: Early brooms were just bundles of twigs tied together.
Medieval Europe: Brooms became more standardized in shape but were still handmade.
1797: The modern broom was popularized in the U.S. by Levi Dickinson, who made one using sorghum tassels (broomcorn).
Over time, broom-making evolved into an industry, and synthetic bristles began replacing natural fibers in the 20th century.
